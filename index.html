Hi, I'm Haseeb, a recent Computer Science graduate from LUMS (May 2026) working in efficient, generalizable, and interpretable machine learning. My research focuses on model compression, domain generalization, and mechanistic interpretability, with an emphasis on understanding failure modes in modern neural networks and designing more robust learning systems.

I work as a Research Assistant at the Centre for Urban Informatics, Technology and Policy (CITY) at LUMS under Dr. Muhammad Tahir and Dr. Zubair Khalid. My core focus is using mechanistic interpretability tools to analyze failure modes in distributed and domain-shift settings. Recently, our work demonstrating that Federated Learning (FedAvg) performance drops under data skew stem from representational misalignment was accepted at the ICML 2026 Mechanistic Interpretability Workshop. I am also researching the Backbone Contrastive Pruning (BaCP) framework to mitigate representational collapse in highly sparse networks.

Previously, I contributed to research at the Computer Vision & Graphics Lab, where I developed KLAWQ, a GPTQ-inspired quantization method improving perplexity by up to 30% over vanilla GPTQ, and engineered a transformer-based framework for single-image camera calibration. I was also selected for the UIUC Summer Research Internship 2025, working with Dr. Darko Marinov and Dr. Reyhaneh Jabbarvand on LLMs for automated software engineering and code translation.

Outside of purely academic research, I work with NEXA as a Founding Product Engineer building scalable distributed ML systems. In the past, I have worked with DatacurveAI, 10x Engineers, and Innova Tech on open-source libraries, diffusion quantization, and edge device deployment.
